Madiba Nelson Mandela

The Man Who United a Divided People

Here is a timeline of President Mandela's life

  • 1918: Born in Mvezo, South Africa.
  • 1944: Joins the African National Congress (ANC).
  • 1952: Opens South Africa’s first Black law firm.
  • 1962: Arrested and later sentenced to life imprisonment.
  • 1964–1990: Imprisoned for 27 years (mostly on Robben Island).
  • 1990: Released from prison.
  • 1993: Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize.
  • 1994: Becomes South Africa’s first Black President.
  • 1999: Steps down as President.
  • 2013: Passes away at age 95.

I have fought against white domination, and I have fought against black domination. I have cherished the ideal of a democratic and free society in which all persons live together in harmony and with equal opportunities.

It is an ideal which I hope to live for and to achieve. But if needs be, it is an ideal for which I am prepared to die.

— Nelson Mandela, Rivonia Trial Speech (1964)
